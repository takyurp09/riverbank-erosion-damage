# Visual Gallery

This repository includes selected public-facing visuals that demonstrate the remote-sensing workflow, physical erosion diagnostics, and economic damage-accounting logic.

## Included Figures

| File | What it demonstrates | Public-release role |
|---|---|---|
| `figures/selected/workflow_overview.svg` | End-to-end flow from Earth Engine exports to erosion panels and damage accounting | Shows reproducible project organization |
| `figures/selected/damage_accounting_framework.svg` | Conceptual link from satellite-observed erosion to damage channels | Shows how geospatial measurement becomes applied economic accounting |
| `figures/selected/damage_components_by_year.png` | Selected national damage components over time | Shows communication of economic damage outputs |
| `figures/selected/gross_vs_net_erosion.png` | Difference between gross land loss, accretion, and net erosion | Shows quality-control thinking around physical erosion measurement |
| `figures/selected/dsas_retreat_rate_by_river_system.png` | DSAS-style retreat-rate comparison across river systems | Shows validation-style shoreline-retreat diagnostics |

## Why Only Selected Visuals Are Included

The full project contains generated maps, robustness figures, and manuscript outputs. This public portfolio release includes only a curated visual set while excluding large intermediate products and complete unpublished output folders.

## Reviewer Takeaway

The visual set is meant to signal three capabilities relevant to remote sensing and applied climate-risk research:

- using satellite products to measure physical environmental change;
- translating geospatial change into economic exposure and damage accounts;
- documenting uncertainty and validation through transparent diagnostics.
